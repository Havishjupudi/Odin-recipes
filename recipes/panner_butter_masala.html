Panner Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala, also known as Paneer Makhani, is a popular North Indian vegetarian dish. It consists of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cubes cooked in a rich and creamy tomato-based gravy. The gravy is typically flavored with a blend of spices, butter, cream, and sometimes cashew nuts to create a flavorful and mildly spicy dish. Paneer Butter Masala is known for its creamy texture and a balance of tangy and mildly sweet flavors, making it a favorite in Indian cuisine. It is often served with naan, roti, or rice.

Ingredients

  • Paneer (cottage cheese)
  • Yogurt
  • Red chili powder
  • Turmeric powder
  • Salt
  • Butter
  • Oil
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Tomatoes
  • Cashew nuts
  • Garam masala
  • Coriander powder
  • Cumin powder
  • Sugar (optional)
  • Heavy cream
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Marinate 250g paneer cubes in 2 tbsp yogurt, 1/2 tsp red chili powder, 1/4 tsp turmeric powder, and a pinch of salt. Set aside.
  2. In a pan, heat 2 tbsp butter and 1 tbsp oil. Add finely chopped onion, minced garlic, and minced ginger. Saut√© until golden.
  3. Add 2 large tomatoes (pureed) and 1/4 cup soaked cashew nuts. Cook until the oil separates.
  4. Add 1/2 tsp each of garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, 1/4 tsp turmeric powder, and 1/2 tsp sugar (optional). Season with salt. Cook for a few minutes.
  5. Blend the mixture into a smooth paste.
  6. Return the paste to the pan, add marinated paneer, and 1/2 cup heavy cream.
  7. Simmer for a few minutes, then add 1 tbsp kasuri methi.
  8. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with naan or rice.
