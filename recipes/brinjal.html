Brinjal Masala Curry

Baingan Masala Curry, also known as Brinjal Masala Curry, is a popular Indian vegetarian dish. It features tender pieces of brinjal (eggplant) cooked in a flavorful and spiced tomato-based gravy. The curry is typically seasoned with a blend of aromatic spices and herbs, including cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala, which gives it a rich and robust flavor. Baingan Masala Curry can vary in spiciness and can be customized to suit different preferences. It's often served with rice or Indian bread (such as roti or naan) and makes for a delicious and comforting meal in Indian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Brinjal (Eggplant)
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Green chilies
  • Cumin seeds
  • Coriander powder
  • Turmeric powder
  • Garam masala
  • Red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Oil for cooking

Instructions

  1. Wash and cut the brinjal (eggplant) into small pieces. Soak them in salted water to reduce bitterness and set aside.
  2. Chop the tomatoes, onions, garlic, ginger, and green chilies.
  3. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter.
  4. Add chopped onions and saut√© until they turn translucent.
  5. Stir in garlic, ginger, and green chilies, and cook for a couple of minutes.
  6. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate.
  7. Season with coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  8. Drain and add the brinjal pieces to the pan. Mix to coat them with the spice mixture.
  9. Cover and cook the brinjal until they become tender, stirring occasionally.
  10. Once the brinjal is cooked, garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  11. Serve the Baingan Masala Curry hot with rice or Indian bread.
